Tribute to MS Dhoni

The journey of a legend: From debut to retirement.

MS Dhoni gearing up before a match.

MS Dhoni, born on July 7, 1981, in Ranchi, India, is a cricket legend known for his cool demeanor, strategic leadership, and finishing abilities. Below is a timeline of some key milestones in his iconic career:

  • 2004: MS Dhoni made his One Day International (ODI) debut against Bangladesh.
  • 2007: He was appointed captain of the Indian T20 team and led India to victory in the inaugural ICC T20 World Cup in South Africa, defeating Pakistan in the final.
  • 2008: India became the No. 1 Test team in the world under his leadership.
  • 2009: Dhoni became the captain of the Indian ODI team, and India retained its position as the No. 1 ranked team.
  • 2011: MS Dhoni hit the match-winning six in the ICC Cricket World Cup final, leading India to their second World Cup victory after 28 years.
  • 2013: MS Dhoni became the first captain in cricket history to win all three ICC trophies â€” ICC T20 World Cup, ICC World Cup, and ICC Champions Trophy.
  • 2014: Dhoni stepped down as captain of the Indian Test cricket team after the Boxing Day Test against Australia.
  • 2017: MS Dhoni stepped down as captain of India's ODI and T20 teams, handing over the reins to Virat Kohli.
  • 2020: On August 15, MS Dhoni announced his retirement from international cricket, leaving behind an extraordinary legacy.
  • 2021: Dhoni led Chennai Super Kings to win their fourth Indian Premier League (IPL) title, cementing his legacy as one of the greatest captains in cricket.

MS Dhoni's contribution to Indian cricket is immeasurable. His leadership, calmness under pressure, and ability to finish matches have made him one of the most beloved figures in the sport.
